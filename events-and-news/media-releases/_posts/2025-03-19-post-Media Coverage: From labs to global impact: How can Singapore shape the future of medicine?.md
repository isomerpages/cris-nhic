<h3>Translating innovations and breakthroughs into global healthcare solutions requires more than just cutting-edge technology.</h3>
<hr>
<p><strong>The commentary by Dr Pauline Tay, Director, National Health Innovation Centre Singapore (NHIC)</strong>,
highlights the importance of deeper collaboration between clinicians, researchers,
investors, corporates, and philanthropies, both locally and globally, to
create a strong foundation for translating innovations and breakthroughs
into global healthcare solutions.</p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/Resources/ST_article_1.png">
</div>
<p>Strengthening investor ties, attracting both private and public partners,
and aligning innovators with hospital networks will further support clinical
expansion, test-bedding, distribution, and global adoption. Overall, sustainable
progress will require collective commitment and investment from all stakeholders
to jointly develop solutions for critical healthcare challenges worldwide.</p>

</p>
